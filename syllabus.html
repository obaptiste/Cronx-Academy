📚 Subjects & Focus Areas

English Language & Literature

Comprehension, creative and critical writing, speech and debate. Read diverse authors; join local poetry events or Croydon Poet Laureate workshops.

Mathematics & Applied Maths

Core topics: algebra, geometry, statistics. Use real-world tasks like budgeting and map reading. Resources: Oak National Academy, Corbettmaths, Khan Academy.

Science (Biology, Chemistry, Physics)

Hands-on experiments and fieldwork. Explore ecosystems in local parks and visit the Science Museum. Keep a digital lab journal.

Modern Languages

Daily practice with Duolingo or BBC Bitesize. Integrate music lyrics, recipes, and films for immersion. End each week with a mini conversation challenge.

Humanities (History, Geography, RE, Citizenship)

Study local history like Croydon’s aviation and music heritage. Discuss global issues – migration, climate, culture – linking them to personal values and responsibility.

Computing & Digital Skills

Programming (Python or Scratch), website creation, and digital citizenship. Build a portfolio project such as a simple blog or app prototype.

Art, Design & Technology

Creative expression through drawing, craft, and digital media. Design eco-friendly projects – upcycling, solar gadgets or community posters.

Physical Education & Wellbeing

At least two hours weekly activity – walks, dance, swim or team sport. Include mindfulness and nutrition sessions to build healthy habits.

Financial Education & Entrepreneurship

Budgeting, saving and ethical spending. Use Bank of England resources (*EconoME*). Plan a small enterprise – for example selling hand-made items or digital art.

🌼 Optional Enrichment & Projects

  • Environmental studies – climate science and gardening.
  • Philosophy & Critical Thinking – debates and logic games.
  • Music & Performance – join Croydon youth choirs or Stanley Arts workshops.
  • Coding & AI – build a chatbot or data-visual project.
  • Community Service – volunteer at libraries or gardens.
